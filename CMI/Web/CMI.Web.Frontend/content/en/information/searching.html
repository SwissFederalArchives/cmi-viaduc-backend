Searching

www.recherche.bar.admin.ch offers a wide range of functions you can use to search for, order and consult documents from the Federal Archives.

Login

You can use the Federal Archives’ online access service anonymously, as a registered user or as an identified person. Subject to status, you will be able to use different functions and a more extensive database for your search. For more information see Registration and identification.

Search options

Simple search

To use the simple search on the homepage enter a term of your choice. All the available data (metadata and full text of digitised documents) will then be searched.

Advanced search

The advanced search allows you to carry out targeted searches in selected data fields (e.g. title, reference code, file reference or full text).
The simple and advanced searches both allow for wildcards “?” and “*” and other operators. A complete list of these operators can be found in “Details of syntax”.

Archive plan

The documents in the Federal Archives are arranged according to a hierarchical structure in the archive plan. This normally reflects the categorisation used when the dossiers were created. Searching in the archive plan can help you to better understand the context of a dossier and find dossiers on similar topics. You can either search directly in the archive plan or switch to the archive plan from a search result.

Thematic overview

A thematic overview is available for documents of the Federal State from 1848 (Main Department E). The fonds are grouped into the following topics: state, people and authorities; foreign policy; justice and police; education, science and culture; national defence; finances; environment, transport, energy and communications; health, work and social security; economy/economic affairs. This overview is a particularly good place to start your search. You can switch from the thematic overview to the archive plan at any time.

Restricting your search: filtering

When you use the simple and advanced search, you receive a list of hits which you can then refine using a number of filters. The filters allow you to display results for specific periods, selected hierarchical levels (e.g. fonds or dossiers), according to accessibility (freely accessible or subject to a closure period), or archival materials that are already available in digital form.

Zeige Details zur Suchsyntax

Syntax

  • (en!) Benutzen Sie OR, um alle Resultate mit dem einen oder dem anderen Suchbegriff zu finden.
    e.g.: Bundesrat OR Bundespräsident

  • Use brackets to group operators.
    e.g.: (Bundesrat OR Bundespräsident) AND Parlament

  • Use NOT to exclude individual terms.
    e.g.: NOT Parlament

  • Place quotation marks around a phrase to search for that exact phrase.
    e.g.: "Basler Übereinkommen"

  • Use * or ? to replace one or more letters (not supported at the start of a word).
    e.g.: Bundes*, Me?er

  • Search for similar but not identical words using the fuzzy operator
    (en!) Bsp., um «Bundesrat», «Bundesräte», «Bundesrats» etc. zu finden: Bundesrat~

  • Use proximity searches. The number indicates the maximum number of words between the terms.
    e.g.: "Professor Einstein" ~5

  • Use ^ to increase the weighting of a word.
    (en!) Bsp., um «Parlament» doppelt so hoch zu gewichten, wie «Bundesrat»: Bundesrat Parlament^2
